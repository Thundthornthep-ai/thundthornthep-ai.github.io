Mergers & Acquisitions 101 — A Practical Guide

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

When a business reaches a certain stage, there are two paths to growth — organic growth or acquisition (M&A). Acquiring a business is faster: you get customers, a team, and technology immediately. But without understanding the legal structure, you may be buying problems instead of opportunities.

Asset Deal vs Share Deal

FactorAsset DealShare Deal
What you buySelected assets and contracts onlyAll or part of the shares — you get the entire company
LiabilitiesYou do not assume existing liabilities (selective)All company liabilities are assumed
LicensesMust reapply (most are non-transferable)Licenses remain with the company
EmployeesMust rehire (may trigger severance obligations)Employees remain with the company
TaxTransfer taxes on each assetShare transfer tax (may be exempt)
ComplexityHigher (transfer item by item)Simpler (transfer shares in one step)

The M&A Process

1. NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)

Both parties sign a confidentiality agreement before sharing sensitive business information.

2. LOI / MOU

A preliminary agreement outlining indicative price, deal structure, key conditions, and the DD timeline. Generally non-binding, except for NDA and exclusivity provisions.

3. Due Diligence

Comprehensive investigation of legal, financial, tax, and operational matters. DD findings shape price negotiations and contract terms.

4. SPA / APA

The definitive agreement containing: purchase price and payment terms, representations and warranties, conditions precedent, indemnification provisions, and post-closing restrictions.

5. Closing

Transfer of ownership, payment of consideration, and completion of all conditions precedent — such as share transfer registration and director changes.

Statutory Mergers Under Thai Law

CCC Section 1238 provides that two or more limited companies may amalgamate by special resolution. Under Section 1240, the merging companies must jointly prepare a new memorandum of association and articles. Section 1243 states that the new company assumes all rights, duties, and liabilities of both predecessor companies.

Practical Tip: Choosing between an asset deal and a share deal depends on multiple factors — existing liabilities, licenses, tax implications, and key contracts. Consult both a lawyer and tax advisor before deciding on the structure.

Conclusion

M&A is a powerful growth accelerator, but it requires a rigorous process from initial negotiation through closing. Every step carries legal, tax, and financial implications that demand expert guidance.
