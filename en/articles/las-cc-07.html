Indemnification Clauses — The Risk Transfer Mechanism

Indemnity is not just "compensation" — it is the most sophisticated risk allocation tool in a contract

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | 3 April 2026 | Legal Advance Solution Co., Ltd.

Indemnity vs Damages

Damages are compensation claimed directly from the breaching party under CCC Section 222, which provides that recoverable damages are those that "ordinarily arise from non-performance" or that "could have been foreseen."

Indemnity is a contractual promise by one party (Indemnitor) to hold the other (Indemnitee) harmless from specified losses, including third-party claims. Unlike damages, indemnity can cover losses not directly caused by the Indemnitor's breach.

"Damages look backward — what happened? Indemnity looks forward — if something happens, who bears the cost?"

Control Mechanisms: Basket, Cap, De Minimis

MechanismMeaningExample
Basket (Deductible)Minimum aggregate threshold before indemnity claims can be madeBasket = THB 1M: if loss is THB 800K, no claim
CapMaximum aggregate indemnity liabilityCap = 50% of purchase price
De MinimisMinimum per-item threshold to count toward the BasketDe Minimis = THB 100K: items below this are ignored
Thai Law Note: While Thai law does not have specific indemnification provisions like common law, parties may freely agree on indemnity terms under the principle of freedom of contract. Under CCC Section 438, courts may determine tort damages by considering the circumstances and severity of the wrongful act.

Negotiation Strategies

For the Indemnitor (Seller / Service Provider)

  • Negotiate a high Basket and low Cap to limit exposure
  • Set a De Minimis to filter trivial claims
  • Define a Survival Period limiting the duration of indemnity obligations
  • Make indemnity the exclusive remedy (Exclusive Remedy clause)

For the Indemnitee (Buyer / Client)

  • Negotiate a low or "First Dollar" Basket (claim from the first dollar once threshold is met)
  • Carve out Fundamental R&W from the Cap
  • Include set-off rights against amounts owed to the Indemnitor
  • Require clear Third-Party Claim Procedures
