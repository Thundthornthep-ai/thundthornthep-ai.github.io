Commercial Lease — 10 Must-Check Points Before Signing

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

You pay tens of thousands in monthly rent, yet the lease agreement is a two-page document nobody reviewed. This is the most common mistake business owners make.

Legal Framework

Lease of property is governed by Sections 537 to 571 of the Thai Civil and Commercial Code (CCC). Section 537 defines a lease as a contract whereby the lessor agrees to let the lessee use or enjoy the benefit of property for a limited period, in exchange for rent.

The 3-Year Rule: Under CCC Section 538, a lease of immovable property for more than 3 years must be made in writing and registered with the competent official. Otherwise, it is enforceable only for 3 years.

10 Must-Check Points

#IssueWhat to Check
1Lease termClear start and end dates; registration required if over 3 years (Sec. 538)
2RentAmount, payment schedule, method, annual escalation (if any)
3Security depositAmount, return conditions, timeline, deduction rules
4Renewal rightsWhether renewal is available, conditions, how new rent is calculated
5Common area chargesCAM fees, water, electricity, maintenance — who is responsible
6RepairsLessor handles major repairs (Sec. 550); lessee handles minor (Sec. 553)
7Alterations & fit-outPermission requirements; ownership of improvements at lease end
8TerminationGrounds, notice period, early termination penalties
9SublettingSubletting is prohibited by default (Sec. 544) unless the lessor consents
10Handback conditionsRequired condition at return, damages assessment, inspection process

Special Considerations

Transfer of Leased Property

Under CCC Section 569, if ownership of the leased property is transferred, the lease remains binding. The new owner assumes all rights and obligations of the original lessor, provided there is clear evidence of the lease.

Termination of Indefinite Leases

Under CCC Section 566, if no fixed term is specified, either party may terminate by giving notice of at least one rental period in advance, but not exceeding two months.

Conclusion

A well-drafted commercial lease covers all 10 points clearly. Never leave important terms to "mutual understanding" — when disputes arise, unwritten agreements become indefensible gaps.
