Exit Strategy — Plan Your Way Out from Day One

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

Every business that starts must think about the exit from day one — not because you plan to fail, but because having a clear exit path is part of sound planning. Every investor wants to know "how do I get out?" before deciding to invest.

Four Primary Exit Routes

Exit RouteDescriptionBest For
IPOPublic offering of shares. Highest potential valuation.Companies large enough with consistent profitability and readiness for public disclosure
Trade SaleSelling shares or assets to a strategic buyerCompanies with specific value (technology, client base, brand)
MBO (Management Buyout)Management team purchases shares from existing shareholdersCompanies with a capable management team seeking ownership
LiquidationWind up the company and distribute assets to shareholdersLast resort when the business cannot continue operations

Company Dissolution Under Thai Law

CCC Section 1236 provides that a limited company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of: (1) any event specified in the articles, (2) expiration of a fixed term, (3) completion of a specific undertaking, (4) a special resolution to dissolve, or (5) bankruptcy.

Upon dissolution, Section 1247 requires a liquidation process. The liquidator must settle the company's affairs, collect assets, pay debts, and distribute remaining assets to shareholders.

Exit Mechanisms in Shareholders' Agreements

1. Drag-Along

When a majority shareholder agrees to sell to a buyer, they can compel minority shareholders to sell their shares on the same terms and conditions. This prevents minority shareholders from blocking a whole-company sale.

2. Tag-Along

Protects minority shareholders. If a majority shareholder sells their shares, minority shareholders have the right to sell their shares alongside on the same terms. This prevents minorities from being stranded with an unknown new majority shareholder.

3. Shotgun Clause

One party names a price to buy the other's shares. The other party must choose: sell at that price, or buy the first party's shares at the same price. This mechanism forces both parties to propose a fair price, since they may end up on either side of the transaction.

Practical Tip: A good exit strategy should be embedded in the SHA from the beginning. Define: (1) events that trigger an exit, (2) valuation methodology, and (3) timeline for execution. Without these provisions, exiting a business becomes a prolonged battle.

Conclusion

An exit strategy is not a sign of failure — it is a sign of professionalism. Businesses that plan their exit from day one are prepared for every outcome, whether that means a profitable sale, a public listing, or an orderly wind-down.
