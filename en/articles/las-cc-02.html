The Definitions Clause — Heart of Every Contract

Definitions are not just a glossary — they are the operating system that runs the entire contract

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | 3 April 2026 | Legal Advance Solution Co., Ltd.

Why Definitions Matter More Than You Think

Most people skip past the definitions section of a contract, treating it as a mere glossary. This is a critical mistake. Definitions are the foundation upon which every operative clause, every obligation, and every risk allocation rests. A single word change in a definition can shift millions of baht in liability.

Consider the term "Material Adverse Change" (MAC). If defined broadly, a buyer in an acquisition can walk away from the deal for almost any deterioration in the target's business. If defined narrowly with carve-outs, the seller is protected from buyer's remorse. The definition alone can determine whether a deal closes or collapses.

"Control the definitions and you control the contract. Everything else is commentary."

Key Definitions Every Business Should Scrutinize

1. Business Day

Seemingly simple, yet crucial for deadlines. Does it include Saturdays? Does it follow Thai public holidays or the counterparty's country? An unclear definition can delay payments and deliveries by days or weeks.

2. Material Adverse Change / Effect (MAC/MAE)

The most heavily negotiated definition in M&A transactions. Broad MAC definitions favor buyers; narrow definitions with industry, market, and regulatory carve-outs favor sellers.

3. Confidential Information

Defines the scope of secrecy obligations. Too narrow and sensitive information leaks without remedy. Too broad and ordinary business information becomes subject to burdensome restrictions.

4. Permitted Transferee / Affiliate

In joint ventures and shareholder agreements, who counts as an "Affiliate" determines whether shares can be transferred within a corporate group without triggering transfer restrictions or rights of first refusal.

Practical Tips

How to Review Definitions

  1. Read definitions before operative clauses — understand the language first
  2. Cross-reference every defined term — check how it is used throughout the contract
  3. Watch for "including but not limited to" — this expands a definition beyond the listed examples
  4. Check for circular definitions — Term A defined by reference to Term B, which references Term A

Caution

Under Thai law, if a contractual definition conflicts with a mandatory statutory definition, the statutory definition prevails. Always verify that your contractual definitions do not contradict the Thai Civil and Commercial Code or relevant special legislation.
