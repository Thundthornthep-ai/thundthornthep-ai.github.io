Business Insurance Law Essentials

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

Factory fire, goods damaged in transit, a customer lawsuit — no one knows when disaster will strike. Business insurance is the "last shield" against unexpected losses. This article explains the essential insurance law principles every business owner should know.

Insurance Contract (CCC Section 861)

The Civil and Commercial Code Section 861 defines an insurance contract as one in which a person agrees to indemnify or to pay a sum of money in the event of loss occurring or of any other future event as specified in the contract, and another person agrees to pay a sum called premium.

Insurable Interest (CCC Section 863)

Section 863 provides that if the insured has no insurable interest in the subject matter insured, the contract is not binding. You must have a genuine financial stake in the insured property to purchase insurance for it.

Types of Business Insurance

TypeCoversSuitable For
Fire InsuranceFire, lightning, explosion, aircraft, natural disastersAll businesses with physical premises
Third-Party LiabilityDamage caused to third parties from business operationsFactories, shops, hotels, restaurants
Cargo InsuranceGoods damaged or lost during transportationImport-export, wholesale, retail
Professional Indemnity (PI)Losses from professional service errorsConsultants, architects, engineers, doctors
Directors & Officers (D&O)Personal liability of directors and officersLimited companies, especially those with multiple shareholders
Duty of Disclosure: CCC Section 865 provides that if at the time of contracting, the insured knew of material facts and failed to disclose them — facts that would have caused the insurer to demand a higher premium or to refuse the contract — the insurance contract is voidable. Full and honest disclosure is mandatory.

Conclusion

Business insurance is a critical risk management tool. The Non-Life Insurance Act B.E. 2535 regulates insurance companies, while the CCC governs insurance contracts. Business owners should select insurance appropriate to their business type and read policy conditions thoroughly before purchasing coverage.
