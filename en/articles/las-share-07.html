Share Transfer in Thai Limited Companies — Steps and Restrictions

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

Transferring shares in a Thai limited company is not as simple as transferring money — there are legal procedures, articles restrictions, and tax consequences to consider.

Legal Basis

Under Section 1108(4) of the Thai Civil and Commercial Code (CCC), a limited company may issue only registered shares. Share transfers must therefore follow the statutory procedure.

Transfer Procedure

1. Check the Articles of Association

Under CCC Section 1129, paragraph one, registered shares are freely transferable unless the articles of association provide otherwise. Always review the articles first.

2. Execute a Transfer Document

Under CCC Section 1129, paragraph two, the transfer must be made in writing, signed by both the transferor and transferee, with at least one witness. The document must specify the transferee's name, the number of shares, and share certificate numbers.

3. Record in the Share Register

The transfer is not enforceable against the company or third parties until it is recorded in the company's share register, with the transferee's name and address (CCC Section 1129, paragraph three).

4. File Updated Shareholder List with DBD

After the transfer, the company must file an updated shareholder list (Bor Gor 5) with the Department of Business Development (DBD).

Transfer Restrictions

Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

The articles may require that before transferring shares to an outsider, the shareholder must first offer them to existing shareholders. This restriction is lawful under CCC Section 1129, paragraph one.

Board Approval

The articles may also require board approval for share transfers. However, the board must have reasonable grounds for any refusal.

Tax Implications

Tax TypeDetails
Personal income taxCapital gains from share transfers are assessable income under Section 40(4)(g) of the Revenue Code
Corporate income taxWhere the transferor is a juristic person, gains are included in corporate income
Stamp dutyThe transfer instrument is subject to stamp duty at 1 Baht per 1,000 Baht (or fraction thereof) of the paid-up value or transfer price of the shares
Warning: Transferring shares at below fair market value may trigger a Revenue Department investigation and tax assessment based on market price.

Conclusion

Every step of a share transfer must be properly executed — from checking the articles, executing the written instrument, recording in the share register, to filing with the DBD. Missing any step may render the transfer incomplete or unenforceable against third parties.
