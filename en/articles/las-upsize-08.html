IP Protection for Growing Businesses

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

For growing businesses, intellectual property is often the most valuable asset — brand names, logos, formulas, software, and designs. Yet many businesses neglect IP protection until infringement occurs, by which point it is often too late.

Four Types of IP Every Business Should Know

TypeWhat It ProtectsGoverning LawDuration
TrademarkNames, logos, slogans distinguishing goods/servicesTrademark Act B.E. 2534 (1991)10 years (renewable indefinitely)

Trademarks — The First Priority

Under Section 6 of the Trademark Act B.E. 2534, a registrable trademark must be distinctive, not prohibited by law, and not identical or confusingly similar to marks already registered by others. Registration grants the holder exclusive rights to use the mark for the designated goods or services.

IP Assignment in Employment — The Most Common Pitfall

The most frequent issue arises when employees or freelancers create works for a company without clear IP ownership agreements.

Copyright

Under Section 9 of the Copyright Act B.E. 2537, copyright in works created under employment belongs to the employee, unless otherwise agreed. The employer only has the right to publish the work for the purpose of the employment. Therefore, if you want copyright to vest in the company, the employment contract must explicitly state this.

Patents

Under Section 11 of the Patent Act B.E. 2522, the right to apply for a patent for an invention created under an employment contract belongs to the employer, unless the contract provides otherwise. This is the opposite of the copyright default.

Practical Tip: Every employment contract, service agreement, and freelancer engagement should contain explicit IP assignment clauses. Specify that all IP created during the engagement — including source code, design files, and know-how — belongs to the company.

Conclusion

Intellectual property represents the true value of a modern business. Registering IP early and having clear IP assignment provisions in every contract protects your most valuable assets from loss.
