Anti-Corruption Compliance 101

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

Your company may face criminal liability even if the directors knew nothing about the bribery. This is not a hypothetical scenario but the actual law in force. The Organic Act on Anti-Corruption B.E. 2561 holds juristic persons liable when associated persons commit corruption for the entity's benefit.

Section 123/5 — Corporate Criminal Liability

The Organic Act on Anti-Corruption B.E. 2561, Section 123/5, provides that if a person associated with a juristic person gives, offers, or promises to give property or any benefit to a government official to induce the official to act, refrain from acting, or delay any act for the benefit of that juristic person, the juristic person shall be guilty of the offence unless it can prove that it had adequate internal control measures in place.

Adequate Procedures

To establish a defence under Section 123/5, a juristic person must demonstrate appropriate internal controls:

  • Anti-Corruption Policy: Written policy communicated to all personnel within the organization
  • Risk Assessment: Identification of corruption risk points in business processes, especially interactions with government agencies
  • Internal Controls: Segregation of duties, multi-level approval processes, and regular audits
  • Training: Regular training for all levels on anti-corruption policy and legal consequences
  • Due Diligence: Background checks on agents, consultants, and third parties acting on behalf of the company
  • Whistleblower Channel: A safe, confidential internal reporting channel for suspected corruption

Whistleblower Protection

Section 36 of the Organic Act provides witness and whistleblower protection measures. Organizations with effective whistleblower systems can detect and address problems earlier, reducing potential damage.

Penalties: Juristic persons found guilty under Section 123/5 face fines of 1 to 2 times the damages caused or benefits received. Courts may also order temporary or permanent business suspension.

Conclusion

Compliance is not only for large corporations. SMEs that interact with government agencies face equal risk. Implementing adequate internal control measures not only protects the company from criminal liability but also builds a transparent and sustainable corporate culture.
