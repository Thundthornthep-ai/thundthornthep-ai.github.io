Conditions Precedent Explained

The gatekeepers of every deal — nothing happens until the CPs are satisfied

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | 3 April 2026 | Legal Advance Solution Co., Ltd.

What Are Conditions Precedent?

Conditions Precedent (CPs) are conditions that must be satisfied before a contract becomes effective or before a party is obligated to perform. They are the "gates" that control when obligations activate. In M&A transactions, CPs determine when a deal closes; in loan agreements, they determine when funds are disbursed.

CCC Section 183: A juristic act subject to a condition precedent takes effect only when the condition is fulfilled. This means that until every CP is satisfied (or waived), the contract's obligations remain suspended.

Under CCC Section 184, if a party dishonestly prevents the fulfillment of a condition, the condition is deemed fulfilled. Conversely, if a party dishonestly causes a condition to be fulfilled, the condition is deemed not fulfilled. This anti-manipulation rule protects the integrity of the CP mechanism.

"CPs are not formalities — they are the last line of defense before you are committed."

Practical Tips

CP Drafting Best Practices

  • Be specific — "Regulatory approval" is vague; "BOI promotion certificate under the Investment Promotion Act B.E. 2520" is precise
  • Set a Long Stop Date — a deadline by which all CPs must be satisfied, failing which either party can walk away
  • Include waiver rights — allow the benefiting party to waive a CP that is for its benefit
  • Allocate responsibility — specify which party is responsible for satisfying each CP

Caution

If a CP is within one party's control (e.g., board approval), that party has an implied obligation to use best efforts to satisfy it. Deliberately failing to obtain your own board's approval to escape a deal may trigger CCC Section 184's anti-manipulation rule and expose you to liability.
