PDPA Compliance Checklist for SMEs

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

Does your business collect customer data? If yes, you are subject to the PDPA. Even a small SME that collects names, phone numbers, or email addresses falls under the Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562, which applies to all organizations regardless of size. Maximum penalties reach 5 million baht.

10-Point PDPA Checklist for SMEs

  1. Create a Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) — Section 39 requires data controllers to maintain records documenting what data is collected, from whom, for what purpose, how long it is retained, and who receives it.
  2. Draft a Privacy Policy — Section 23 requires informing data subjects before or at the time of collection about purposes, data types, retention period, rights, and contact channels.
  3. Implement Consent Management — Section 19 requires consent from data subjects for data collection. Consent must be in writing or electronic form, clearly separated from other terms, in plain language, and revocable at any time.
  4. Handle Sensitive Data Carefully — Section 26 requires explicit consent for processing sensitive data including race, political opinions, religion, sexual behavior, criminal records, health data, biometric data, and genetic data.
  5. Execute Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) — Section 40 requires written agreements with data processors (cloud providers, CRM systems, delivery companies) specifying duties and security measures.
  6. Implement Security Measures — Section 37(1) requires appropriate security measures to prevent loss, unauthorized access, use, alteration, or disclosure of personal data.
  7. Prepare a Breach Response Plan — Section 37(4) requires notifying the PDPC within 72 hours of discovering a data breach. If high risk to individuals, data subjects must also be notified.
  8. Define Data Retention Periods — Establish clear retention periods by purpose. When no longer necessary, data must be deleted or anonymized per Section 37(3).
  9. Establish Data Subject Rights Procedures — Sections 30-36 grant rights of access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, and objection. SMEs must have channels and processes to handle these requests.
  10. Appoint a Responsible Person — While small SMEs may not need a formal DPO, designate someone to oversee PDPA compliance within the organization.
Getting started: Begin with items 1 (ROPA) and 2 (Privacy Policy) as they form the foundation for understanding what data your organization collects. Then expand to other items in order.

Conclusion

PDPA compliance is not as daunting as it seems, but it requires action. SMEs that implement all 10 items will be well-positioned to protect customer data and reduce the risk of complaints or fines.
