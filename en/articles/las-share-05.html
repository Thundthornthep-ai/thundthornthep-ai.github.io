Guarantee Contracts — What Every Guarantor Must Know

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

A single signature on a guarantee contract can make you liable for someone else's entire debt. Thai law, however, was significantly amended in 2014 and 2015 to strengthen guarantor protections.

What Is a Guarantee Contract?

Under Section 680 of the Thai Civil and Commercial Code (CCC), a guarantee (suretyship) is a contract whereby a third party (the guarantor) binds themselves to the creditor to perform the obligation if the debtor fails to do so. A guarantee must be evidenced in writing to be enforceable (Section 680, paragraph two).

Statutory Rights Protecting Guarantors

1. Right to Be Notified of Default (Sec. 686)

The creditor must notify the guarantor within 60 days when the debtor defaults. If the creditor fails to give notice, the guarantor is not liable for interest or damages accruing after the 60-day period.

2. Right to Early Payment (Sec. 691/1)

The guarantor may pay the guaranteed debt at any time, even before it becomes due.

3. Right of Recourse (Sec. 693)

After paying on behalf of the debtor, the guarantor has a right of recourse against the debtor for the full amount paid.

4. Right of Subrogation (Sec. 693)

The guarantor who has paid the debt is subrogated to all of the creditor's rights, including rights over any collateral securing the obligation.

6 Void Contractual Terms (Sec. 681/1)

Section 681/1 of the CCC (as amended in 2014) renders the following terms void:

#Void TermRationale
1Making the guarantor liable as a joint debtorContradicts the accessory nature of suretyship
2Requiring the guarantor to pay before the creditor enforces against the debtorViolates the guarantor's right to require enforcement against the principal debtor first
3Making the guarantor liable for more than the guaranteed debtGuarantor should not bear a burden exceeding the principal obligation
4Requiring the guarantor to waive statutory rightsStatutory protections are matters of public policy
5Allowing the creditor to extend time for the debtor without the guarantor's consentChanged conditions directly affect the guarantor's exposure
6Any term contradicting the protective provisions on suretyshipGuarantor protection is a public policy matter
Key Point: These terms are void regardless of what the contract says. The guarantor may raise them as a defense, and courts will not enforce such provisions.

Checklist Before Signing

  • Is the guaranteed amount clearly specified? (Sec. 681)
  • Is there a maximum liability cap?
  • Does the contract contain any terms void under Section 681/1?
  • Is the creditor required to notify you upon the debtor's default?
  • Duration of guarantee — perpetual or fixed term?

Conclusion

Acting as a guarantor means assuming someone else's financial risk. The 2014 and 2015 amendments to Thai law significantly strengthened guarantor protections, but guarantors must understand their rights to benefit from them. Before signing, always review the terms carefully and seek legal advice.
