Non-Disclosure Agreement Essentials

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

Once confidential information leaks, it cannot be recalled. This is why an NDA should be the first document signed before sharing any sensitive business information with partners, investors, or new employees. Yet an incomplete NDA may offer no protection at all.

Trade Secrets Under Thai Law

The Trade Secrets Act B.E. 2545, Section 3, defines a "trade secret" as commercial information that is not generally known, has commercial value because it is secret, and for which the controller has taken appropriate measures to maintain secrecy. Section 6 defines infringement as unauthorized disclosure, acquisition, or use, including breach of contract or duty of confidentiality.

Essential NDA Components

1. Definition of Confidential Information

Clearly specify what constitutes confidential information, covering written, oral, electronic, and other forms. A definition too broad may be narrowed by courts; one too narrow may fail to cover critical data.

2. Scope of Obligations

Define the recipient's duties: (1) non-disclosure to third parties, (2) use only for the agreed purpose, (3) maintenance of confidentiality with measures no less stringent than those used for their own information.

3. Duration

Typically 2-5 years. For certain trade secrets, obligations may continue for as long as the information remains confidential.

4. Four Standard Exceptions

  • Public Domain: Information already publicly available or becoming public without fault of the recipient
  • Prior Knowledge: Information the recipient already knew before receiving it
  • Independent Development: Information independently developed by the recipient
  • Third Party Disclosure: Information received from a third party with legal right to disclose

5. Remedies for Breach

Effective NDAs must specify injunctive relief, damages claims, and liquidated damages. Under CCC Section 379, courts may reduce excessive penalties.

Practical tip: Unilateral NDAs protect only the disclosing party. Mutual NDAs protect both sides. If both parties exchange information during negotiations, use a Mutual NDA.

Conclusion

An NDA is the first line of defense for business information, but must contain all essential components to be enforceable. A generic NDA copied without customization may prove worthless when a dispute arises.
