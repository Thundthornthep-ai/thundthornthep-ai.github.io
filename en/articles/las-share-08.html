Power of Attorney — Legal Requirements in Thailand

Thundthornthep Yamoutai, Ph.D. | LAS Legal | 3 April 2026

You cannot attend a government office in person, so you send someone on your behalf. But if the Power of Attorney is defective, the official will reject it on the spot.

Legal Framework — Agency Law

A Power of Attorney (POA) is governed by the law of agency under Sections 797 to 814 of the Thai Civil and Commercial Code (CCC). Section 797 provides that an agent is a person authorized to act on behalf of a principal, and acts performed by the agent within their authority bind the principal.

Required Elements

  • Principal's details — Name, address, ID number
  • Attorney's details — Name, address, ID number
  • Scope of authority — Clear description of what is authorized
  • Date — Date of execution
  • Principal's signature — Must be signed personally
  • Witnesses — At least 1 (recommended 2)
  • Stamp duty — Affixed at the prescribed rate

Key Considerations

1. Written Form Required for Certain Acts

Under CCC Section 798, paragraph two, where the law requires a transaction to be in writing, the appointment of an agent to perform that transaction must also be in writing. Examples include real property transfers and mortgage registrations.

2. Agent Must Act Within Authority

Under CCC Section 812, if the agent acts beyond their authorized scope, the principal is not bound — unless the principal ratifies the act. The scope of authority should therefore be clear but not excessively broad.

3. Termination of Agency

Under CCC Section 826, the agency terminates upon: death of either principal or agent, either party becoming incapacitated or bankrupt, or revocation by the principal.

Stamp Duty Rule: A POA without proper stamp duty cannot be admitted as evidence in civil proceedings under Section 118 of the Revenue Code. Always ensure stamp duty is affixed.

Conclusion

A valid Power of Attorney requires a clear scope of authority, all mandatory elements, and proper stamp duty. Getting it right from the start saves time and prevents legal complications down the road.
